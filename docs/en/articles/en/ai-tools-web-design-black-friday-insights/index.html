AI Tools and Web Design: Gentle Lessons from Black Friday’s Digital Shift


The recent Black Friday and Cyber Monday sales in the US have delivered some compelling insights into the shifting landscape of eCommerce. The retail data confirms a significant trend: Artificial Intelligence (AI) is becoming an increasingly helpful element in the online shopping experience.

This US trend gently signals the direction of how customers might soon expect to interact with digital storefronts globally. It offers a valuable opportunity to reflect on what this emerging pattern could mean for designing digital platforms.

The Steady Shift to Online Buying

The headline figures show a strong preference for digital channels during the key shopping period:

  • - Record Online Spending: US online sales on Black Friday reached a record $11.8 billion, showing a 9.1% growth year-over-year.
  • - Continued Growth: Cyber Monday was projected to drive $14.2 billion in sales, reinforcing its position as the largest online shopping day of the year.


Physical store traffic was relatively subdued. With many customers focused on value amid tighter budgets, they appear to be choosing the most efficient channels for securing deals, often bypassing the traditional crowds.

AI as a New Digital Helper

The most significant insight from the data is the growing role of AI as a supportive tool. Reports indicate that AI-driven traffic to retail sites soared by over 800% compared to the previous year.

AI tools, such as large language models (LLMs) used in chatbots like Walmart’s Sparky or Amazon’s Rufus, are helping customers in critical ways:

  • - More Efficient Discovery: AI helps make the product search and gift discovery process “quicker and more guided,” easing the common stress of finding the right items.
  • - Support for Comparison: These tools are being used to quickly compare prices and secure discounts.
  • - Influencing Sales: Globally, AI agents influenced billions of dollars in online sales on Black Friday, demonstrating their impact on consumer choices.


This suggests that AI is evolving into a valuable digital helper, guiding the shopper’s journey rather than just serving as a simple search bar.

Nuances of Consumer Behaviour

Beyond the growth figures, the reports offer interesting details on how people are spending:

  • - Price Sensitivity: Despite the spending surge, consumers purchased fewer items at checkout (units per transaction fell 2%), suggesting greater price sensitivity.
  • - Higher Average Price: Average selling prices rose 7%. This increase was attributed to factors like trade tariffs pushing up product costs and the strong purchasing power of higher-income earners, evidenced by growth in the luxury categories.
  • - Value Erosion: For many, promotions may not have felt as deep due to underlying higher product costs (inflation and tariffs). This means the real value of the bargains may have slipped for the average consumer.

Reflections on Web Design

Observing these trends allows us to reflect on how our digital platforms can better support the AI-assisted shopper. We can learn from the large retailers by adopting simple, thoughtful design principles:

1. Focus on Clarity and Performance

If AI is a primary tool for discovery, the platform’s fundamentals must be strong:

  • - Site Performance: Fast load times are fundamental for ensuring a smooth experience for both human visitors and automated tools seeking information.
  • - Clear Information Architecture: Product details, pricing, and specifications need to be easily scannable and accessible. Transparency in information builds user trust.

2. Consider Smart Assistance

We can make information easier to find and consume:

  • - Thoughtful Search: Ensure your site’s internal search function is robust and helpful, offering relevant suggestions or corrections.
  • - Simple Q&A Tools: A well-designed, structured FAQ or a simple Q&A tool can significantly improve the user experience by providing quick, accurate answers, supporting the shopper’s need for speed.

3. Design for a Welcoming Journey

The interface design should feel supportive and welcoming, complementing the guided nature of AI assistance:

  • - Clear Pathways: Use unambiguous Call-to-Actions (CTAs) and headings to gently guide the user through the service or product offerings.
  • - Streamlined Checkout: The path from initial interest to purchase should be intuitive and require minimal friction.

Takeaway


Black Friday shows us that online shoppers increasingly value guidance as much as choice. Thoughtful web design—fast, clear, and intuitive—creates space for AI and other supportive tools to enhance the experience. By focusing on simplicity, transparency, and seamless journeys, we can meet evolving expectations while quietly preparing for the future of AI-assisted shopping.
