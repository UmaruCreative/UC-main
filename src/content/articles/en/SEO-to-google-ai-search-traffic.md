## The new search reality

Something big is happening in how people find information online — and it’s quietly rewriting the rules of visibility.  

Google’s AI-driven search results, where answers appear directly on the results page, are changing user behaviour at scale. Fewer people are clicking through to websites — even when no AI summary is shown.

Recent data from Seer Interactive shows:
- **Organic click-through rates have dropped by 61%**
- **Paid search clicks fell by 68%**
- Even results **without AI summaries saw a 41% decline** in clicks  


![Illustration of CTR on Queries with No AIO Present have dropped 41%, image from seerinteractive ](/images/No-AIO-Seerinteractive.png) 
Source: Seer Interactive


For many brands, it feels like the familiar flow of traffic has suddenly slowed to a trickle.

---

## From search engine to “answer engine”

Google is no longer just a gateway — it’s becoming the destination.  

Instead of directing users to websites, Google now serves AI-generated answers directly within search results. The goal: faster answers, less friction.  

But for website owners, this means less visibility and fewer opportunities for engagement. In effect, Google has “brought the answers home” — and kept the traffic for itself.

---

## The new business model behind the shift

There’s a pattern emerging.  
1. **Cut the free exposure:** AI summaries reduce clicks, eroding organic visibility.  
2. **Introduce a new solution:** As brands grow anxious about lost traffic, Google unveils embedded ads within AI-generated answers.  
3. **Sell the key back:** To be seen again, brands must now pay to pass through Google’s new gates.  

It’s a bold business move: redefine the flow of attention, then sell access back to it.

---

## Quality and trust take centre stage

To fuel these AI answers, Google needs reliable sources. That’s where **E-E-A-T** — Experience, Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness — becomes critical.  

AI no longer just scans for **keyword density** — it now evaluates which content is most credible and worth citing.  
This shift rewards brands that share real experience, genuine insights, and trustworthy voices over those that optimise only for algorithms.

For small businesses and creators, this is an opportunity — not just to rank, but to be *quoted* by AI systems. The internet is moving from “who ranks highest” to “who is most credible”.

---

## How to adapt your strategy

The game isn’t over. It’s just changing.  

Here’s where to focus next:  
- **Rebalance your ad strategy:** Allocate part of your budget to AI-integrated ad formats and high-intent keywords.  
- **Protect transactional searches:** Maintain SEO for purchase-ready queries — these still feed AI models and drive conversions.  
- **Upgrade your content:** Create pieces that offer insight, proof, and expertise. Write for *humans first*, but structure for machines.  

The new SEO is less about gaming algorithms — and more about building digital authority.

---

## Takeaway

As Google shifts from showing links to showing answers, brands built on **genuine expertise** will remain visible — because **credibility now defines discovery**.
